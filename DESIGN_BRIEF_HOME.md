# Homepage High Fidelity Design Brief

## Project Goal
- Build an international, high quality game discovery and decision platform.
- Combine corporate credibility, content depth and user conversion into one homepage experience.
- Use the homepage to route users from search or direct visits into games, platforms, comparisons, guides and business cooperation.
- Support Google-driven organic acquisition by giving users clear paths into SEO content and high-intent decision pages.

## Homepage Goal
- Communicate within the first screen that the website helps users discover games, explore platforms, compare options and learn before choosing.
- Establish a premium, trustworthy game-focused brand impression.
- Provide clear entry points for four primary user actions: Find Games, Explore Platforms, Compare and Learn.
- Convert qualified business visitors through a visible but non-intrusive Business Cooperation path.
- Balance gaming atmosphere with decision clarity, editorial trust and product-level polish.

## User Types
- General players looking for new games or platforms.
- New players who need simple guidance before choosing.
- Experienced players comparing platforms, games or alternatives.
- Search users arriving from Google through game, platform, guide or comparison keywords.
- Business partners, platforms, advertisers or channels looking for cooperation.

## Page Structure
- Navigation: brand on the left, core navigation links in the center, Business Cooperation as a high-intent action, mobile menu for small screens.
- Hero Section: strong headline, concise value proposition, primary CTA, secondary CTA and premium game-related hero visual.
- Core Discovery Cards: four entry cards for Find Games, Learn, Compare and News.
- Featured Platforms: selected platform cards that guide users toward platform detail or comparison flows.
- Why Choose Us: trust statements such as Independent, Transparent, Secure and User Focused.
- Evaluation Method: explain the platform's criteria, including Real Experience, Data Driven, Security First and Updated Regularly.
- Business Cooperation Banner: clear B2B conversion area for platform owners and partners.
- Footer: navigation, support, legal, social and language information.

## Visual Requirements
- Overall look: premium dark gaming product with international editorial credibility.
- First screen: immersive but readable, with the next section slightly visible below the fold.
- Color: dark foundation, controlled bright accent for primary actions and consistent neutral text hierarchy.
- Imagery: high quality game-related visuals showing gameplay, platforms, screens, controllers or game worlds.
- Cards: compact, premium, information-focused, with consistent radius, subtle borders, clear title and action link.
- Typography: modern, confident and readable, with strong headline hierarchy and restrained body copy.
- Layout: stable grid, deliberate spacing, high contrast and fast scanning across desktop and mobile.
- Brand quality: avoid cheap promotion, generic template feel, casino-like presentation or noisy decorative effects.

## Interaction Goals
- Users can immediately choose a path without reading the whole page.
- Primary CTA leads to the main exploration path.
- Secondary CTA supports lower-commitment learning or methodology understanding.
- Discovery cards route users into major content areas.
- Featured platform cards encourage deeper evaluation and platform selection.
- Trust blocks reduce hesitation before users click into recommendations, comparisons or inquiries.
- Business Cooperation remains available from navigation, mid-page banner and footer.

## Review Standards
- The homepage clearly communicates game discovery and decision-making within the first screen.
- The visual direction feels international, premium, trustworthy and game-relevant.
- The page structure matches the approved DESIGN.md architecture and does not add unrelated sections.
- The four main user paths are visible: Find Games, Explore Platforms, Compare and Learn.
- Business Cooperation is present without distracting from ordinary user browsing.
- CTA hierarchy is clear, with primary, secondary and business actions visually distinct.
- Cards and content blocks are scannable, consistent and aligned with the platform's trust positioning.
- The design avoids generic SaaS visuals, low-trust affiliate styling, gambling/casino cues and excessive decoration.
- The brief remains design-only and does not specify implementation technology or development work.
